Tdap vaccine

The Tdap vaccine is typically given as a booster dose to adolescents and adults to provide continued protection against these diseases. It is especially important for pregnant women to receive the Tdap vaccine during each pregnancy to protect both themselves and their newborns from pertussis. Additionally, individuals should receive a tetanus booster every 10 years throughout adulthood to maintain immunity against tetanus.

Hib vaccines

The Hib vaccine is highly effective in preventing Hib infection and its associated diseases. It works by stimulating the body's immune system to produce antibodies against the Hib bacteria. By doing so, the vaccine helps protect individuals, especially infants and young children, from developing severe illnesses caused by Hib.

Pneumococcal Vaccine

The pneumococcal vaccine has been instrumental in reducing the incidence of pneumococcal infections and their associated complications, including hospitalizations and deaths. Routine vaccination with the pneumococcal vaccine is recommended for individuals of all ages, particularly those at increased risk of pneumococcal disease, to help protect against these serious infections

MMR vaccine

Measles is a viral disease spread by respiratory droplets or airborne transmission that causes a widespread rash and can lead to encephalopathy (brain damage). Mumps is a viral disease that causes the painful inflammation of the salivary glands and can also affect the pancreas, testicles, and brain. Rubella (German measles) is a viral disease that affects the skin, lymph nodes, and joints. It can cause birth defects in babies if an infection occurs during pregnancy.

Rotavirus Vaccine

Rotavirus is a viral infection that can cause severe gastroenteritis (stomach flu) in infants and children, leading the dehydration and, in some cases, death. Rotavirus is the most common cause of childhood diarrhea worldwide. Roughly 80% of rotavirus deaths occur in the developing world.12 Prior to the development of a rotavirus vaccine, an estimated three million cases occurred in the United States every year
